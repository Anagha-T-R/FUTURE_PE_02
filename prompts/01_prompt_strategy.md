# Prompt Strategy

## Objective

Generate UGC-style ad content that feels natural, specific, and conversion-focused for a D2C skincare product.

## Product Context

**Brand:** GlowNest  
**Product:** Barrier Repair Gel Cream  
**Category:** D2C skincare  
**Audience:** Students and working professionals aged 18-30 with sensitive, dry, or irritated skin  
**Primary promise:** Calm, lightweight hydration that supports the skin barrier without feeling sticky  
**Conversion action:** Visit product page, buy now, or save the routine for later

## Audience Pain Points

- Skin feels tight after washing
- Moisturizers feel heavy or greasy
- Sensitive skin reacts to complicated routines
- Dry patches show under makeup or sunscreen
- People want simple skincare that fits busy mornings

## Brand Voice

- Honest
- Warm
- Practical
- Friendly
- Not overly clinical
- Not exaggerated or miracle-based

## Prompt Logic

The prompt workflow separates strategy from output generation. This improves quality because the model first understands the customer and product before creating scripts.

### Stage 1: Context Setup

The model receives the product, customer, pain points, brand tone, and platform goals.

### Stage 2: Hook Ideation

The model creates several hook styles:

- Pain point hook
- Curiosity hook
- Mistake hook
- Before-after hook
- Routine hook
- Social proof hook

### Stage 3: Script Writing

Each script follows a simple UGC structure:

1. Hook
2. Relatable problem
3. Product introduction
4. Benefit demonstration
5. Proof or personal experience
6. CTA

### Stage 4: Platform Adaptation

The same core idea is adjusted for:

- Instagram Reels: fast, visual, punchy
- Facebook Ads: slightly clearer product education
- YouTube Shorts: strong opening and direct watch-through pacing

## Quality Rules

- Use simple spoken language.
- Avoid medical claims.
- Focus on believable benefits.
- Keep scripts short enough for video execution.
- Include visual direction for creators.
- Make CTAs specific and low-friction.
